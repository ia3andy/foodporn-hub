# 🔥 Tomahawk Steak & Frites — The Ultimate Carnivore Indulgence

> **"So juicy, so spicy — it's pure foodporn."**

When it comes to unapologetic meat worship, nothing beats a perfectly seared tomahawk steak. Thick, marbled, bone-in, and
glistening with flavor — this primal cut is a feast for the eyes and the senses. Pair it with golden, crispy frites and you've
got a plate that screams *luxury meets comfort food*.

---

## 🥩 The Star: Tomahawk Steak

The tomahawk steak gets its name from the long rib bone that resembles a Native American tomahawk. It’s essentially a ribeye —
but turned up to 11.

### Why it's special:

- **Flavor**: Generous marbling means every bite is rich and buttery.
- **Presentation**: That bone isn’t just for looks. It insulates the meat while cooking, giving you juicier results.
- **Drama**: Let’s be real. This isn’t just a steak. It’s a showstopper.

Cooked rare to medium-rare, the steak should have a charred crust, a tender pink center, and enough juice to make your cutting
board weep with joy.

---

## 🍟 On the Side: Frites Maison

Crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside — the perfect French fries are a labor of love. Double-fried for extra crunch,
dusted with sea salt, and served piping hot, they cut through the richness of the steak like a dream.

*Optional: Drizzle with truffle oil if you want to take things dangerously over the top.*

---

## 🌶️ A Touch of Spice

This dish doesn't just seduce your taste buds — it teases them. The addition of chili flakes or a fresh red pepper on the side
brings a playful heat that lingers after each bite. Not enough to burn, but just enough to make your mouth say, *"Wait... what
was that?"*

---

## 🧂 The Finish

Seasoned simply with coarse salt, cracked black pepper, and a hint of garlic or rosemary, this tomahawk doesn’t need much. The
meat does all the talking — you’re just here to listen... and chew.

## 🔥 Pro Tips:

- Let your steak rest at room temp for 30 minutes before cooking.
- Use a cast iron skillet or blazing grill for that sear.
- Always rest the steak after cooking — 10 minutes minimum. It’s like foreplay, but for meat.
